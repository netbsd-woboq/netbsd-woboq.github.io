106const char *const moves[] = {
107 "\nMoves and Points:",
108 "\n Moves are made along the positions on the board according to",
109 "their numbers. Red moves in the positive direction (clockwise",
110 "from 1 to 24), and white moves in the negative direction (coun-",
111 "terclockwise from 24 to 1).",
112 "\n A turn consists of rolling the dice, and moving the number of",
113 "positions indicated on each die. The two numbers can be used to",
114 "move one man the sum of the two rolls, or two men the number on",
115 "each individual die. For example, if red rolled 6 3 at the start",
116 "of the game, he might move a man from 1 to 7 to 10, using both",
117 "dice for one man, or he might move two men from position 12, one",
118 "to 15 and one to 18. (Red did not have to choose two men start-",
119 "ing from the same position.) In addition, doubles are treated",
120 "specially in backgammon. When a player rolls doubles, he gets to",
121 "move as if he had four dice instead of two. For instance, if you",
122 "rolled double 2's, you could move one man eight positions, four",
123 "men two positions each, or any permutation in between.",
124 "",
125 "\n However, there are certain limitations, called 'points.' A",
126 "player has a point when he has two or more men on the same posi-",
127 "tion. This gives him custody of that position, and his opponent",
128 "cannot place his men there, even if passing through on the way to",
129 "another position. When a player has six points in a row, it is",
130 "called a 'wall,' since any of his opponent's men behind the wall",
131 "cannot pass it and are trapped, at least for the moment. Notice",
132 "that this could mean that a player could not use part or all of",
133 "his roll. However, he must use as much of his roll as possible.",
134 "",

139 "\n The most important part of the game is removing men, since",
140 "that is how you win the game. Once a man is removed, he stays",

143 "the last six positions of the board (19-24 for red, 6-1 for",
144 "white).",
145 "\n To get off the board, a player must roll the exact number to",

161const char *const hits[] = {
162 "\nHitting Blots:",
163 "\n Although two men on a position form an impenetrable point, a",
164 "lone man is not so secure. Such a man is called a 'blot' and has",
165 "the potential of getting hit by an opposing man. When a player's",
166 "blot is hit, he is placed on the bar, and the first thing that",
167 "player must do is move the man off the bar. Such moves are",
168 "counted as if the bar is one position behind the first position",
169 "on the board. Thus if red has a man on the bar and rolls 2 3, he",
170 "must move the man on the bar to position 2 or 3 before moving any",
171 "other man. If white had points on positions 2 and 3, then red",
172 "would forfeit his turn. Being on the bar is a very bad position,",
173 "for often a player can lose many turns trying to move off the",
174 "bar, as well as being set back the full distance of the board.",
175 "",
1760};
